Liedl et al. (2005) – Analytical Plume Model

The Liedl et al. (2005) model provides an analytical expression for the maximum length of a reactive groundwater plume in a vertically non-uniform aquifer. It accounts for aquifer thickness, transverse dispersivity and a stoichiometric reaction between an electron donor and an electron acceptor.

In this tool you provide:

  • M – aquifer thickness (m)
  • αTv – transverse dispersivity (m)
  • γ – stoichiometric ratio
  • CEA° – initial electron acceptor concentration (mg/L)
  • CED° – initial electron donor concentration (mg/L)

The current implementation uses a simplified expression for the maximum plume length (compute_liedl_Lmax in analytical_routes.py). Later you can replace that with the exact formula ported from the original CAST implementation while keeping the same UI.

Reference:
Liedl, R. et al. (2005). Steady-state plumes in vertically nonuniform aquifers. Journal of Contaminant Hydrology.
